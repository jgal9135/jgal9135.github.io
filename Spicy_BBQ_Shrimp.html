Spicy BBQ Shrimp

Ingredients

2 cups all-purpose flour plus additional for dusting pan

1 teaspoon baking soda

3/4 teaspoon salt

1/2 teaspoon baking powder

1 cup walnuts (3 1/2 oz), toasted

2 teaspoons cinnamon

1 1/4 cups sugar

1 1/2 sticks of unsalted butter (3/4 cup), softened, plus 2 tablespoons melted

2 large eggs at room temperature

1 cup sour cream

1 teaspoon vanilla

Photo courtesy of Culinaria

Servings: 4 to 8

Active time: 30 min

Total Time: 2 hours 30 mins

Directions

Will need a 10-cup bundt pan (10 inches in diameter)

1. Preheat oven to 350Â°F. Generously butter bundt pan and dust with flour, knocking out excess.

2. Whisk together flour, baking soda, salt, and baking powder in a bowl.

3. Pulse walnuts, cinnamon, 1/4 cup sugar, and a pinch of salt in a food processor until nuts are chopped. Add melted butter and pulse once to combine.

4. Beat together remaining 1 1/2 sticks butter and remaining cup sugar in a large bowl with an electric mixer at high speed until pale and fluffy, about 3 minutes in a standing mixer or 5 minutes with a handheld. Reduce speed to medium and add eggs 1 at a time, beating well after each addition, then beat in sour cream and vanilla until smooth and fluffy, about 2 minutes more in a standing mixer or 4 minutes more with a handheld. Reduce speed to low and add flour mixture, scraping down side of bowl occasionally and mixing until just combined.

5. Sprinkle half of nut mixture evenly in bottom of bundt pan. Spoon half of batter over nuts and smooth with a rubber spatula. Sprinkle batter in pan with remaining nut mixture and top with remaining batter, smoothing top. Bake in middle of oven until cake begins to pull away from side of pan and a tester comes out clean, 45 to 50 minutes.

6. Cool cake in pan on a rack 1 hour, then run a thin knife around outer and inner edges. Place rack on top of pen, then flip to invert cake onto rack. Cool completely.
